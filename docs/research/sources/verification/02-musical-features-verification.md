## Issues to fix

Only BROKEN / MOVED / MISMATCH / APPROXIMATE items, with the specific correction.

1. **Freitas et al. 2018 — MISMATCH (most serious).** §8 and the master table claim familiar music "reliably engages reward and emotion circuitry" and that "familiarity is one of the strongest predictors of an emotional response (neuroimaging meta-analysis, Freitas et al. 2018)." The paper found the **opposite**: a motor activation pattern (superior frontal gyrus, ventral-lateral thalamus, SMA) and explicitly "not much evidence that limbic engagement was modulated by familiarity" (only 11 studies, 212 subjects, non-significant after correction). **Fix:** Remove the Freitas citation as support for the reward/emotion claim, or rewrite to "familiarity engages motor/audio-motor synchronization networks; limbic/reward modulation was not robust (Freitas et al. 2018)." The general "familiarity increases liking / mere-exposure" claim should be re-sourced (it is not what Freitas shows).

2. **Salimpoor "willingness to pay" — APPROXIMATE / wrong-paper attribution.** §3 attributes "Accumbens activity predicted how much money people would pay" to Salimpoor et al. **2011**. That finding is from Salimpoor et al. **2013, *Science*** ("Interactions Between the Nucleus Accumbens and Auditory Cortices Predict Music Reward Value," doi:10.1126/science.1231059). The 2011 paper only shows the caudate/accumbens anticipation-vs-peak dissociation. **Fix:** Either add the 2013 Science cite for the money/valuation sentence, or attribute it to the PNAS review already in References.

3. **"Di Stefano et al. 2022" — author MISMATCH.** §4 / References attribute "Register impacts perceptual consonance through roughness and sharpness" to "Di Stefano et al." The actual authors are **Tuomas Eerola & Imre Lahdelma** (Durham), *Psychon. Bull. Rev.* 2022. **Fix:** Correct author to "Eerola & Lahdelma 2022." Also add open PMC mirror: `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9166839/` (the link.springer URL 303-redirects to an auth gate).

4. **Mütze/Kopiez/Wolf entrainment review — date MISMATCH.** §1 body and master table call it a "2015 review" / "review 2015," but the cited paper is **2018** (Musicae Scientiae; online 2018, issue 2020). **Fix:** Change "2015" to "Mütze, Kopiez & Wolf 2018." (Finding itself is correctly characterized.)

5. **Bretherton 2019 — n APPROXIMATE + numbers UNVERIFIABLE.** The study enrolled/allocated **58 participants** (per the abstract/search), not the **n=52** stated. The specific figures **BRS 19.09 vs 15.27 ms/mmHg**, **η² 0.06–0.25**, and **R² 0.15–0.17** could not be confirmed from any openly accessible text (SAGE renders nav-only to the fetcher). **Fix:** Verify n against the full PDF (52 may be the completed-analysis subset; state it as such, e.g. "n=52 of 58 enrolled") and re-confirm the BRS/η²/R² numbers against the open-access full text before relying on them; otherwise soften to "increased baroreflex sensitivity at slow tempo" without the exact ms/mmHg values.

6. **Van Dyck 2015 URL — MOVED.** `sportsmedicine-open.springeropen.com/...` 301-redirects to `link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40798-015-0025-9` (then an auth gate for fetchers). **Fix:** Update URL to the link.springer canonical, or cite the open PMC version. The ±2% cadence / ±3% tempo figures remain UNVERIFIABLE from accessible text and should be re-checked against the full open-access paper.

7. **432 Hz cancer-trial URL — auth gate.** `link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12906-025-04758-5` 303-redirects to an idp.springer auth gate. The numbers are correct. **Fix (optional):** Cite the open PMC mirror `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11755923/` for a clean open link.

8. **Acoustic-startle PMC8655082 — APPROXIMATE fit.** This paper is about motor response-triggering (SCM EMG, reaction time) scaling with stimulus intensity — it confirms the acoustic startle reflex exists and scales with intensity, but does **not** document the cardiovascular ↑HR/BP response or the "~110 dB classic elicitor" figure the doc cites it for. **Fix:** Soften the §5 sentence, or add a dedicated cardiovascular-startle reference for the HR/BP and 110 dB claims.
