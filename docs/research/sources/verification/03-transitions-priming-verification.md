## Issues to fix

- **MISMATCH — Sleep "13 RCTs (N=1,007) moderate-certainty PSQI":** This figure is **misattributed**. It is not found in either cited sleep source (PMC11746032 is a 27-study meta-narrative review; the network MA is 20 trials/N=1,339). The "13 RCTs / 1,007 participants / moderate-certainty PSQI (MD −2.79)" figures come from the **Cochrane review "Listening to music for insomnia in adults" (Jespersen et al., 2022)**, which is **not in the References**. *Fix:* add the Cochrane citation/URL `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9400393/` and attribute the 13-RCT/N=1007 stat to it. (The underlying number is correct — only the citation is missing/wrong.)

- **APPROXIMATE — Section 3, "exciting music in particular boosted performance" (PMC8357712):** The sustained-attention study found music increased *subjective task-focus / reduced mind-wandering* but did **not** significantly improve objective performance (RT / RT variability) vs silence. *Fix:* soften to "improved subjective task-focus / reduced mind-wandering" rather than implying a performance gain.

- **APPROXIMATE — Section 3, "systematic review … confirms arousal as the mediating mechanism" (S0167876024000321):** The review actually concludes physiological evidence is *mixed/insufficient* and that arousal-mood support rests largely on subjective ratings. *Fix:* reword to "is consistent with / proposes arousal as a candidate mediating mechanism (physiological evidence mixed)."

- **APPROXIMATE — Section 7 / Section 4 framing:** "Listening specifically for relaxation outperformed distraction/activation" (PLOS ONE) is drawn from a single EMA study noted in the review, not a meta-analytic comparison. *Fix:* attribute as an observation from one included study, not a pooled finding.

- **MOVED (cosmetic) — four `www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/...` URLs** (PMC8656869, PMC6986749, PMC10669558, PMC9021823) issue 301 redirects to `pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/...`. All resolve fine; optionally update to the canonical `pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov` host for consistency with the other PMC links.

- **APPROXIMATE — date — oxytocin review (fcogn.2025.1678665):** Doc labels it "Frontiers 2025," but the paper's publication year is **2026** (DOI slug says 2025). Minor; correct the year if precision matters.

- **APPROXIMATE — 2026 iso/mood-disorders paper (10.1177/03057356261427384):** Confirmed real and on-topic, but the specific claim that *both* the iso-principle *and* a compensatory (jump-to-target) principle were "suitable, iso being the gentler default" could not be fully verified from the abstract. Recommend confirming against full text before relying on the compensatory-principle detail.
