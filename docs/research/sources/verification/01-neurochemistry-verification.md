## Issues to fix

1. **Salimpoor 2011 open-PDF link is BROKEN** — `http://audition.ens.fr/P2web/eval2011/BT_Salimpoor2011.pdf` fails TLS certificate verification. The underlying claim is fine. Replace with a working mirror or drop the open-PDF parenthetical; the Nature DOI page itself resolves (behind paywall). The finding (caudate=anticipation, NAcc=peak) is CONFIRMED.

2. **Bernardi 2006 URL is the wrong PMC record (MOVED)** — `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1860853/` is the editorial commentary "The sound of silence is music to the heart," not the Bernardi paper. Change to the actual article: `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1860846/`. Finding is CONFIRMED.

3. **"Grahn & Rowe 2009" citation is MISMATCHED to its URL** — `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19673753/` is Grahn (2009) *single-author* review "The role of the basal ganglia in beat perception" (Ann NY Acad Sci 1169:35–45). The real Grahn & Rowe 2009 is "Feeling the Beat: Premotor and Striatal Interactions in Musicians and Nonmusicians during Beat Perception," J Neurosci 29(23):7540–7548, PMID 19515922 / `https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2702750/`. Fix: either relabel the citation to "Grahn 2009" (matching the current URL) or swap the URL to the real Grahn & Rowe paper. The beat-perception finding is supported either way.

4. **2025 Frontiers oxytocin review — claim is APPROXIMATE / slightly overstated** — The doc's "strongest when music is social/participatory, weaker for solitary passive listening" simplifies a review that explicitly stresses BIDIRECTIONAL oxytocin modulation (group singing decreased oxytocin in young adults, increased it in older adults) and that both directions can be adaptive. Soften to "oxytocin response is context-dependent and shaped by activity type / vocal mode" rather than implying social music uniformly raises oxytocin. URL is fine.

5. **Melatonin claim is APPROXIMATE** — The doc states "studies measuring melatonin found no significant change." The cited Front Neurol review (PMC11746032) reports one study with no change (light, not sound, drives melatonin) but ALSO an ICU study where melatonin increased after interactive music therapy. Soften to "melatonin findings are mixed; the sleep benefit is not reliably attributable to melatonin (it tracks arousal reduction)." URL is fine.

6. **~3–5% musical anhedonia prevalence is APPROXIMATE in attribution** — The number is well supported in the literature (Mas-Herrero / Marco-Pallarés ~3–5%, ~5.5% in one sample), so the figure stands. But it is presented within the Martínez-Molina 2016 paragraph, and that paper does NOT state 3–5% (only "a small percentage"). Fix: attribute the prevalence to Mas-Herrero et al. 2013 / Marco-Pallarés et al., not to Martínez-Molina 2016.

7. **Minor — sleep tempo range** — Cochrane review (PMC9400393) describes common intervention tempo as 52–85 BPM; doc says 60–80 BPM. Within range, no action strictly required, but note if precision matters.

(No MISMATCH where the source contradicts a claim outright, except the Grahn & Rowe authorship/URL pairing in item 3. No fully BROKEN reference except the Salimpoor open-PDF mirror in item 1.)
